About the project

“A Place to Call Home” is an immersive documentary project produced as the final project of Orel’s Master’s degree at the Steve Tisch School of Film and Television at Tel Aviv University. The project explores alternative forms of housing created as side effects of the demanding modern life. The project is taking part in the discourse about the meaning of postmodern-age ‘Home’.

During the work on the project, the team met with the psychological functions of the concept ‘Home’, especially with forms of loneliness as the different tenants experience it, and the way it affects their housing choices. Postmodern perceptions and concepts also reflect themselves in the artistic style of the project, especially in the animation work and the textual references.

About me

My name is Orel Shmueli, I am a Digital Media student at the Tel Aviv University and the creator of “A Place to Call Home”. Since I can remember, I always dreamed of designing my own home. I dreamed of having a huge window in the living room which I can see the sunset from. Amazingly, during the work on the project I bought my own home, with a huge window facing west, of course.

I believe home is a reflection of our soul and is an essential part of our self-definition. We design it, organize it, and fill it with items that remind us repeatedly who we are. I invite you to explore yourself through my project, to look around you, at your own home, and to try to figure out who you are through its reflection.
